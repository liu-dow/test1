1. Why You Need a Pet Tracker

According to the American Humane Association, approximately 10 million pets are lost in the United States each year, with only 15% of dogs and 2% of cats without identification ever being reunited with their owners [1]. These statistics highlight the critical importance of having reliable methods to locate your pet if they go missing.

Beyond the emotional distress of losing a pet, there are practical reasons why a GPS tracker is becoming an essential tool for pet owners:

  • Escape artists: Some breeds and individual dogs are natural escape artists who can jump fences, dig under barriers, or slip through small openings.

  • Pet sitters and kennels: When others are caring for your pet, a GPS tracker allows you to verify their location and ensure they're where they should be.

How GPS Pet Trackers Work

GPS pet trackers like the Tractive operate through a three-step process:

  1. Location determination: The device uses GPS satellites to calculate its precise location coordinates.
  2. Data transmission: These coordinates are then transmitted via cellular networks (2G, 3G, 4G, or LTE, depending on the device) to the tracker's servers.
  3. User access: The information is made available to you through a smartphone app or web interface, allowing you to see your pet's location in real-time.

This system allows for accurate tracking anywhere with cellular coverage, which makes GPS trackers like the Tractive ideal for most pet owners in urban, suburban, and many rural areas.

According to a white paper published by the National Institute of Standards and Technology, GPS technology can provide location accuracy within 3-5 meters under optimal conditions [3]. This level of precision is more than adequate for locating a lost pet in most scenarios.

3. Benefits of Pet Trackers

Modern GPS pet trackers like the Tractive offer far more than simple location tracking. Here's what a quality GPS tracker can provide:

Real-Time Location Tracking

The primary function of any GPS tracker is to show you your pet's exact location at any given moment. This is invaluable if your pet escapes or goes missing, allowing you to quickly locate and retrieve them before they wander too far or encounter potential dangers.

Virtual Fences (Geofencing)

Most GPS trackers allow you to set up virtual boundaries or "safe zones." If your pet leaves these designated areas, you'll receive an immediate alert on your smartphone, enabling you to react quickly even if you're not actively monitoring the app.

Activity Monitoring

Many modern trackers, including the Tractive, monitor your pet's activity levels throughout the day. This feature helps you ensure your pet is getting enough exercise and can even alert you to potential health issues if their activity patterns suddenly change.

Location History

GPS trackers typically store your pet's movement history, allowing you to see where they've been throughout the day. This can be particularly useful for understanding their habits or identifying potential escape routes.

Family Sharing

Most tracker apps allow multiple family members to access your pet's location and activity data, making it easier to coordinate search efforts if your pet goes missing or simply to keep everyone in the household informed about your pet's whereabouts.

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) reports that regular physical activity is as important for pets as it is for humans, helping to prevent obesity and associated health problems [4]. The activity monitoring features in modern GPS trackers like the Tractive can help ensure your pet is getting the exercise they need to stay healthy.
