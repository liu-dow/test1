How to Choose the Right GPS Tracker for Your Pet

Choosing the right GPS tracker is crucial for ensuring your pet's safety. Here are some key factors to consider:

Battery Life

Battery life is a crucial factor when choosing a GPS tracker. Longer battery life means less frequent charging and better protection for your pet.

Water Resistance

If your pet loves swimming or plays in the rain, a waterproof tracker is essential. Make sure to choose a water resistance rating suitable for your pet's activities.

Tracking Range

Tracking range determines how far away you can locate your pet. For pets that love to explore, a larger tracking range might be more important.

Frequently Asked Questions

Most GPS trackers require a monthly or annual subscription plan to support cellular connectivity and cloud services. These subscriptions typically cost between $5-15 per month. Some trackers offer subscription-free options but may have limited functionality.

GPS tracker battery life varies greatly, from a few days to several months. Battery life depends on various factors including tracking frequency, device size, and features. Most modern trackers last 1-2 weeks under normal use, while some can last longer in power-saving modes.
